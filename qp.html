Frequency Domain Design (Lag) MAY 2019 / DEC 2019 | 10 Marks

Design a suitable lag compensator for a feedback system with open-loop transfer function $G(s) = \frac{K}{s(1+2s)}$ so that Phase Margin is $40^\circ$ and steady-state error for a ramp input $\le 0.2$.

Step 1: Determine K

Given $e_{ss} \le 0.2$ for a ramp input, Velocity error constant $K_v = \frac{1}{e_{ss}} = \frac{1}{0.2} = 5$.

$$ K_v = \lim_{s \to 0} s G(s) = \lim_{s \to 0} s \frac{K}{s(1+2s)} = K \implies K = 5 $$

Step 2: Uncompensated Phase Margin

Let $G(j\omega) = \frac{5}{j\omega(1 + j2\omega)}$. From the Bode plot, gain crossover frequency $\omega_{gc} \approx 1.15$ rad/sec.

$$ \phi_{gc} = -90^\circ - \tan^{-1}(2 \times 1.15) \approx -156^\circ $$ $$ PM_{uncomp} = 180^\circ - 156^\circ = 24^\circ $$

Step 3: New Gain Crossover Frequency

Desired Phase Margin $\gamma_d = 40^\circ$. Add $\epsilon = 5^\circ$ for safety. New required PM $\gamma_n = 45^\circ$.

$$ \text{Required Phase at new } \omega_{gc}: \phi_{gcn} = -180^\circ + 45^\circ = -135^\circ $$ $$ -90^\circ - \tan^{-1}(2\omega) = -135^\circ \implies \tan^{-1}(2\omega) = 45^\circ \implies \omega_{gcn} = 0.5 \text{ rad/sec} $$

Step 4: Design Compensator Parameters

Calculate magnitude of uncompensated system at new $\omega_{gcn} = 0.5$ rad/sec:

$$ A_{gcn} = \left| \frac{5}{j0.5(1 + j1)} \right| = \frac{5}{0.5 \sqrt{2}} = \frac{10}{\sqrt{2}} \approx 7.07 \implies 20 \log_{10}(7.07) \approx 17 \text{ dB} $$

Using the standard QP graphical approximation of $A_{gcn} = 20$ dB:

$$ 20 \log \beta = 20 \implies \beta = 10 $$ $$ \frac{1}{T} = \frac{\omega_{gcn}}{10} = \frac{0.5}{10} = 0.05 \implies T = 20 $$

Step 5: Final Transfer Function

$$ G_c(s) = \frac{1 + sT}{1 + s\beta T} = \frac{1 + 20s}{1 + 200s} $$

Design a lead compensator for $G(s) = \frac{K}{s(s+8)}$ to satisfy: (1) %OS = 9.5%, (2) $\omega_n = 12$ rad/sec, (3) $K_v \ge 10$.

Step 1: Dominant Pole Location

$$ \%M_p = 9.5\% \implies e^{\frac{-\zeta\pi}{\sqrt{1-\zeta^2}}} = 0.095 \implies \zeta = 0.6 $$ $$ s_d = -\zeta\omega_n \pm j\omega_n\sqrt{1-\zeta^2} = -7.2 \pm j9.6 $$

Step 2: Angle Deficiency

Poles are at $s=0, s=-8$. Angles to $s_d$:

$$ \theta_1 (\text{from } s=0) = 180^\circ - \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{9.6}{7.2}\right) = 126.9^\circ $$ $$ \theta_2 (\text{from } s=-8) = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{9.6}{8-7.2}\right) = 85.2^\circ $$ $$ \angle G(s_d) = -(126.9^\circ + 85.2^\circ) = -212.1^\circ $$ $$ \text{Required Phase Lead: } \phi = 180^\circ - 212.1^\circ = 32.1^\circ \approx 32^\circ $$

Step 3: Finding Pole and Zero (Bisector Method)

Using the geometric bisector method, the angle $APO = 53.1^\circ$. Bisecting it and drawing $\pm 16^\circ$ yields the coordinates on the real axis:

$$ \text{Zero } z_c = -9.1, \quad \text{Pole } p_c = -16.25 \implies G_c(s) = \frac{s + 9.1}{s + 16.25} $$

Step 4: Finding K and Checking $K_v$

Using Magnitude Condition at $s_d = -7.2 + j9.6$:

$$ K \left| \frac{s_d + 9.1}{s_d(s_d + 8)(s_d + 16.25)} \right| = 1 \implies K \approx 150 $$

$$ K_v = \lim_{s \to 0} s G_c(s)G(s) = \frac{150 \times 9.1}{8 \times 16.25} = 10.5 \ge 10 \quad \text{(Satisfied)} $$

Controllability Test from Transfer Function MAY 2019 | 5 Marks

Define controllability and observability of a system and check whether the system $\frac{Y(s)}{U(s)} = \frac{1}{(s+1)(s+2)}$ is controllable or not.

Step 1: Convert to State Space (Phase Variable Form)

$$ \frac{Y(s)}{U(s)} = \frac{1}{s^2 + 3s + 2} \implies \ddot{y} + 3\dot{y} + 2y = u $$

Let $x_1 = y$, $x_2 = \dot{y}$. Then $\dot{x}_1 = x_2$ and $\dot{x}_2 = -2x_1 - 3x_2 + u$.

$$ \begin{bmatrix} \dot{x}_1 \\ \dot{x}_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -2 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} u $$

Step 2: Kalman's Test

$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -2 & -3 \end{bmatrix}, \quad B = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \implies AB = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ Q_c = [B \quad AB] = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \implies |Q_c| = -1 \neq 0 $$
Since $|Q_c| \neq 0$, the system is completely state controllable.
Parametric Controllability Test MAY 2023 | 5 Marks

System is $\dot{X} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & -3 \end{bmatrix} X + \begin{bmatrix} 1 & a \\ 0 & b \end{bmatrix} U$. Find the range of 'a' and 'b' for the system to be controllable.

Step 1: Kalman's Matrix

$$ AB = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & a \\ 0 & b \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & -a \\ 0 & -3b \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ Q_c = [B \quad AB] = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & a & -1 & -a \\ 0 & b & 0 & -3b \end{bmatrix} $$

For controllability, the rank of the $2 \times 4$ matrix $Q_c$ must be 2. There must be at least one $2 \times 2$ minor that is non-zero.

Looking at columns 1 & 2: $\begin{vmatrix} 1 & a \\ 0 & b \end{vmatrix} = b$. Looking at cols 1 & 4: $\begin{vmatrix} 1 & -a \\ 0 & -3b \end{vmatrix} = -3b$.

As long as $b \neq 0$, the rank is 2. The variable $a$ can be any real number.

System $\dot{x} = Ax$ with $A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}$. Solve for $P$ using $Q=I$. Determine stability and write Lyapunov function.

Step 1: Set up Lyapunov Equation

$$ \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} p_{11} & p_{12} \\ p_{12} & p_{22} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} p_{11} & p_{12} \\ p_{12} & p_{22} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} $$

Multiplying and expanding gives three equations:

$$ -2p_{12} = -1 \implies \mathbf{p_{12} = 0.5} $$ $$ -p_{22} + p_{11} - p_{12} = 0 \implies p_{11} - p_{22} = 0.5 $$ $$ 2p_{12} - 2p_{22} = -1 \implies 1 - 2p_{22} = -1 \implies \mathbf{p_{22} = 1} \implies \mathbf{p_{11} = 1.5} $$

Step 2: Check Definiteness & Final Function

$$ P = \begin{bmatrix} 1.5 & 0.5 \\ 0.5 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \quad (\Delta_1 = 1.5 > 0, \Delta_2 = 1.25 > 0) \implies \text{Stable} $$ $$ V(x) = x^T P x = 1.5x_1^2 + x_1 x_2 + x_2^2 $$
Lyapunov Stability Theorem JUL 2021 | 10 Marks

Apply Lyapunov stability to determine the stability of $\dot{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & -12 \\ 7 & -14 \end{bmatrix} x$.

Use $A^T P + P A = -I$:

$$ \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 7 \\ -12 & -14 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} p_{11} & p_{12} \\ p_{12} & p_{22} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} p_{11} & p_{12} \\ p_{12} & p_{22} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & -12 \\ 7 & -14 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} $$

Solving the 3 linear equations yields:

$$ p_{11} = 37/36, \quad p_{12} = -29/36, \quad p_{22} = 61/84 $$

Checking Sylvester's criterion: $\Delta_1 = 37/36 > 0$. $\Delta_2 = (37/36)(61/84) - (-29/36)^2 \approx 0.097 > 0$.

Since P is positive definite, the system is Asymptotically Stable.
Lyapunov Direct Method (Nonlinear) DEC 2019 / SEP 2020 | 10 Marks

Investigate stability: $\dot{x}_1 = x_2$, $\dot{x}_2 = -x_1 - x_1^2 x_2$.

Choose a positive definite Lyapunov function candidate $V(x) = x_1^2 + x_2^2$ (or $0.5x_1^2 + 0.5x_2^2$).

$$ \dot{V} = 2x_1 \dot{x}_1 + 2x_2 \dot{x}_2 = 2x_1 (x_2) + 2x_2 (-x_1 - x_1^2 x_2) $$ $$ \dot{V} = 2x_1 x_2 - 2x_1 x_2 - 2x_1^2 x_2^2 = -2x_1^2 x_2^2 $$

Since $\dot{V} \le 0$, the origin is stable. $\dot{V} = 0$ only along the axes $x_1=0$ or $x_2=0$, and trajectories cross the axes, so it is Asymptotically Stable.
